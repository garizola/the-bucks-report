## Thoughts

I must admit I was not too excited for this first game. After what happened last season, it almost feels impossible to fully trust the team as hope was drained out of every player, coach, and fan. 

I don’t want to get too in the weeds about last season because the 2024-2025 season is officially underway. During the offseason there were a few moves made by GM, Jon Horst. I don’t think any Bucks fans were keen on watching the likes of Taurean Prince, Gary Trent Jr, and Delon Wright play. All have been decent in their respective careers, but not stellar. 

Fast forward to gametime and after watching the first half, you could tell they were going to make a massive impact on the team. Delon Wright’s performance was rather forgettable, but tha, in the best way possible, is fine. Being the 8th or 9th man over A.J. Green and Andre Jackson Jr is an important role. Not that A.J. and Ajax are bad, but having a better player step in and take minutes from them almost seemed like a crucial step towards winning games at the end of last season and into this one. 


Prince and Trent prevailed as big surprises. While they both played slightly lackluster defense, they were solid overall. They each had a USG% under 10.3 and, consequently, posted zero turnovers. Gary Trent Jr. had a massive 34.5 minutes on the court. His PIE was only 3.9, but he passed the eye test. Trent is going to play a similar role to Beasley last season. However, I think most can agree he is certainly an upgrade. Trent went 2/5 from beyond the arc on Tuesday in a catch and shoot type role. Prince on the other hand, while (assumingly) starting in place of Middleton, went 4/5 from three and 2/2 from inside the arc. This led him to put up an 18.3 PIE which was 3rd behind Giannis and Dame. Quite a debut performance.
